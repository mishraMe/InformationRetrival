<p><b>Senftenberg</b> (<a href="/wiki/Sorbian_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Sorbian language">Sorbian</a>: <span lang="wen" xml:lang="wen"><i>Zły Komorow</i></span>) is a town in southern <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Oberspreewald-Lausitz" title="Oberspreewald-Lausitz">Oberspreewald-Lausitz</a> district.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Senftenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Senftenberg is located in the southwest of the historic <a href="/wiki/Lower_Lusatia" title="Lower Lusatia">Lower Lusatia</a> region at the border with <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>. Its town centre is situated north of the river <a href="/wiki/Black_Elster" title="Black Elster">Black Elster</a> and the artificial <a href="/wiki/Senftenberger_Lake" class="mw-redirect" title="Senftenberger Lake">Senftenberger Lake</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Lusatian_Lake_District" title="Lusatian Lake District">Lusatian Lake District</a> chain, approximately 20 kilometres (12&#160;mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Hoyerswerda" title="Hoyerswerda">Hoyerswerda</a>, and 35 kilometres (22&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Senftenberg_station" title="Senftenberg station">Senftenberg station</a> is north of the centre and a major railway freight yard is located to its north-east, with a locomotive depot.</p>

<p>Senftenberg was first mentioned in a 1279 deed issued by <a href="/wiki/Henry_III,_Margrave_of_Meissen" title="Henry III, Margrave of Meissen">Henry III the Illustrious of Wettin</a>, then margrave of <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Lusatia" class="mw-redirect" title="Margraviate of Lusatia">Lusatia</a>. With Lower Lusatia, the settlement was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Kingdom of Bohemia</a> under <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV of Luxembourg</a> in 1368. Elector <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Elector_of_Saxony" title="Frederick II, Elector of Saxony">Frederick II</a> of <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Saxony" title="Electorate of Saxony">Saxony</a> acquired Senftenberg in 1448, whereafter the area as a border stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wettin" title="House of Wettin">House of Wettin</a> was separated from Bohemian Lusatia, until in 1635 all Lusatian territories fell to <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Saxony" title="Electorate of Saxony">Saxony</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Prague_(1635)" title="Peace of Prague (1635)">Peace of Prague</a>. According to the 1815 <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a>, Lower Lusatia was annexed by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Prussia</a> and incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Brandenburg" title="Province of Brandenburg">Province of Brandenburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Senftenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Lake "Senftenberger See"</b></p>
<p>The Senftenberger See (Senftenberger Lake) is a popular destination for both day trips and holiday. In 1973, the former open cast mine, was officially opened to the public. Today, the lake is known for its excellent water quality. It is part of the socalled Lusatian Lakeland, a group of 23 artificial lakes.</p>
