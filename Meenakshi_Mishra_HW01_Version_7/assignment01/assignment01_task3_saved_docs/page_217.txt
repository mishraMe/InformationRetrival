			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Microgram</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Metric_system" title="Metric system">metric system</a>, a <b>microgram</b> or <b>microgramme</b> (<b>μg</b>; the recommended symbol in the United States when communicating medical information is <b>mcg</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Physical_unit" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical unit">unit</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a> equal to one billionth (<span class="nowrap"><span style="display:none" class="sortkey">6991100000000000000♠</span>1<span style="margin-left:0.25em;margin-right:0.15em;">×</span>10<sup>−9</sup></span>) of a <a href="/wiki/Kilogram" title="Kilogram">kilogram</a>, one millionth (<span class="nowrap"><span style="display:none" class="sortkey">6994100000000000000♠</span>1<span style="margin-left:0.25em;margin-right:0.15em;">×</span>10<sup>−6</sup></span>) of a <a href="/wiki/Gram" title="Gram">gram</a>, or one thousandth (<span class="nowrap"><span style="display:none" class="sortkey">6997100000000000000♠</span>1<span style="margin-left:0.25em;margin-right:0.15em;">×</span>10<sup>−3</sup></span>) of a milligram. The unit symbol is <b>μg</b> according to the <a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">International System of Units</a>. In μg the prefix symbol for <a href="/wiki/Micro-" title="Micro-">micro-</a> is the Greek letter <a href="/wiki/Mu_(letter)" title="Mu (letter)">μ (Mu)</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abbreviation_and_symbol_confusion">Abbreviation and symbol confusion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microgram&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Abbreviation and symbol confusion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the Greek lowercase “μ” (Mu) in the symbol μg is typographically unavailable, it is occasionally—although not properly—replaced by the Latin lowercase “u”.</p>
<p>The United States-based Institute for Safe Medication Practices (ISMP) and the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> (FDA) recommend that the symbol μg should not be used when communicating medical information due to the risk that the prefix <a href="/wiki/Micro-" title="Micro-">μ (micro-)</a> might be misread as the prefix <a href="/wiki/Milli-" title="Milli-">m (milli-)</a>, resulting in a thousandfold overdose. The non-<a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">SI</a> symbol <b>mcg</b> is recommended instead.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, the abbreviation mcg is also the symbol for an obsolete <a href="/wiki/CGS" class="mw-redirect" title="CGS">CGS</a> unit of measure known as millicentigram, which is equal to 10&#160;μg.</p>
<p>In the UK, because serious medication errors have been made from the confusion between milligrams and micrograms when micrograms has been abbreviated, the recommendation given in the Scottish Palliative Care Guidelines is that doses of less than one milligram <i>must</i> be expressed in micrograms and that the word ‘microgram’ must be written in full, and that it is never acceptable to use “mcg” or “μg”.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gamma (symbol: <a href="/wiki/%CE%93" class="mw-redirect" title="Γ">γ</a>) is a deprecated non-<a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">SI</a> unit of <a href="/wiki/Mass" title="Mass">mass</a> equal to one microgram.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
