<p>The <b>Parliamentary Commissioner for the Environment</b> (<i>Te Kaitiaki Taiao a Te Whare Pāremata</i> in <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="Māori language">Māori</a>) is an independent Officer of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Parliament" title="New Zealand Parliament">New Zealand Parliament</a> appointed by the Governor-General on the recommendation of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_House_of_Representatives" title="New Zealand House of Representatives">House of Representatives</a> for a five-year term under the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Act_1986" title="Environment Act 1986">Environment Act 1986</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Parliamentary Commissioner for the Environment replaced the Commission for the Environment, a Government agency which was formed in 1972.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliamentary_Commissioner_for_the_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Commissioner is one of three officers of Parliament (the Ombudsmen and the controller and auditor general) who are independent of the executive and who may review activities of the executive government and report directly to Parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Commissioner's role is to review and provide advice on <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environmental</a> issues and the system of agencies and processes established by the Government to manage the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The primary objective of the office is to contribute to maintaining and improving the quality of the environment in New Zealand through advice given to Parliament, local councils, business, <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">tangata whenua</a>, communities and other public agencies.</p>
<p>The Commissioner may:</p>
<ul>
<li>investigate any matter where the environment may be, or has been adversely affected;</li>
<li>assess the capability, performance and effectiveness of the New Zealand system of environmental management; and</li>
<li>provide advice and information that will assist people to maintain and improve the quality of the environment.</li>
</ul>
