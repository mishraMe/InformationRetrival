<p><b>Kamuthi Solar Power Project</b> is a solar photovoltaic power generating station at <a href="/wiki/Kamuthi" title="Kamuthi">Kamuthi</a>, 90 km from Madurai, in the state of <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a>, India.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This project has been commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Adani_Power" title="Adani Power">Adani Power</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With a generating capacity of 648 MW at a single location<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> it is the world's largest single location solar project,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> surpassing the <a href="/wiki/Topaz_Solar_Farm" title="Topaz Solar Farm">Topaz Solar Farm</a> in California, which has a capacity of 550 MW.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The project was completed on 21 September 2016<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> with an investment of around ₹ 4,550 crore (45.5 billion <a href="/wiki/Indian_rupee" title="Indian rupee">Indian rupees</a>, approx US$ 680 million at 2016 rates). The solar plant consists of 2,500,000 solar modules and 27,000 metres of structures. It consist of 576 inverters and 154 transformer and almost 7500 km of cables.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Panels occupy 1270 acres of land. 30,000 tonnes of galvanised steel were used. About 8500 personnel worked on average installing about 11 MW in a day to set up the plant in stipulated time. Given the solar resource of around 5.5 kWh/m<sup>2</sup>,day<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> an annual generation of 1.3 TWh/yr may be possible.</p>
