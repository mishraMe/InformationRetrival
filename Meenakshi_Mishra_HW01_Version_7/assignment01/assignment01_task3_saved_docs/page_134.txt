<p><b>Barstow</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino_County,_California" title="San Bernardino County, California">San Bernardino County</a>, California, United States. The population was 22,639 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>. Barstow is located 55 miles (89&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino" class="mw-redirect" title="San Bernardino">San Bernardino</a>.</p>
<p>Barstow is a major transportation center for the <a href="/wiki/Inland_Empire" title="Inland Empire">Inland Empire</a>. Several major highways including <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_in_California" title="Interstate 15 in California">Interstate 15</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interstate_40" title="Interstate 40">Interstate 40</a>, <a href="/wiki/California_State_Route_58" title="California State Route 58">California State Route 58</a>, and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_66" title="U.S. Route 66">U.S. Route 66</a> converge in the city. It is the site of a large rail <a href="/wiki/Classification_yard" title="Classification yard">classification yard</a>, belonging to the <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a> also runs through town using <a href="/wiki/Trackage_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Trackage rights">trackage rights</a> on BNSF's main line to <a href="/wiki/Daggett,_California" title="Daggett, California">Daggett</a> 10 miles (16&#160;km) east, from where it heads to <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a> and the BNSF heads to Chicago. Barstow is about 62 miles (100&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Baker,_California" title="Baker, California">Baker, California</a> and 111 miles (179&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Primm,_Nevada" title="Primm, Nevada">Primm, Nevada</a>. Barstow is almost exactly midway between Los Angeles, California (130 miles (210&#160;km) southwest) and <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, Nevada (125 miles (201&#160;km) northeast).</p>
<p>Barstow is home to <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Logistics_Base_Barstow" title="Marine Corps Logistics Base Barstow">Marine Corps Logistics Base Barstow</a> and is the closest city to the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Irwin_National_Training_Center" title="Fort Irwin National Training Center">Fort Irwin National Training Center</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The settlement of Barstow began in the late 1830s in the <a href="/wiki/Mormon_Corridor" title="Mormon Corridor">Mormon Corridor</a>. Every fall and winter, as the weather cooled, the rain produced new grass growth and replenished the water sources in the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a>. People, goods and animal herds would move from <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> to Los Angeles, along the <a href="/wiki/Old_Spanish_Trail_(trade_route)" title="Old Spanish Trail (trade route)">Old Spanish Trail</a> from <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_New_Mexico" title="Santa Fe, New Mexico">Santa Fe</a>, or after 1848, on the <a href="/wiki/Mormon_Road" title="Mormon Road">Mormon Road</a> from <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>. Trains of freight wagons traveled back to Salt Lake City and other points in the interior. These travelers followed the course of the Mojave River, watering and camping at <a href="/wiki/Fish_Ponds" title="Fish Ponds">Fish Ponds</a> on its south bank (west of <a href="/wiki/Nebo_Center,_California" title="Nebo Center, California">Nebo Center</a>) or 3.625 miles up river on the north bank, at a riverside grove of willows and cottonwoods, festooned with wild grapes, called <b>Grapevines</b> (later the site of North Barstow). In 1859, the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Road" title="Mojave Road">Mojave Road</a> followed a route was established from Los Angeles to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Mojave" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Mojave">Fort Mojave</a> through Grapevines that linked eastward with the <a href="/wiki/Edward_Fitzgerald_Beale#The_Beale_Wagon_Road_and_Camel_Corps" title="Edward Fitzgerald Beale">Beale Wagon Road</a> across northern <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico_Territory" title="New Mexico Territory">New Mexico Territory</a> to Santa Fe.</p>
<p>Indian troubles with the <a href="/wiki/Paiute#Traditional_Southern_Paiute_bands" title="Paiute">Paiute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mojave_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Mojave people">Mojave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemehuevi" title="Chemehuevi">Chemehuevi</a> tribes followed and from 1860 <a href="/wiki/Camp_Cady" title="Camp Cady">Camp Cady</a>, a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a> post 20 miles (32&#160;km) east of Barstow, was occupied sporadically until 1864, then permanently, by soldiers occupying other posts on the Mojave Road or patrolling in the region until 1871.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Trading posts were established at Grapevines and Fish Ponds that supplied travelers on the roads and increasingly the miners that came into the Mohave Desert after the end of hostilities with the native people.<sup id="cite_ref-landuse_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-landuse-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barstow's roots also lie in the rich mining history of the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a> following the discovery of gold and silver in the <a href="/wiki/Owens_Valley" title="Owens Valley">Owens Valley</a> and in mountains to the east in the 1860s and 1870s. Due to the influx of miners arriving in <a href="/wiki/Calico,_San_Bernardino_County,_California" title="Calico, San Bernardino County, California">Calico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daggett,_California" title="Daggett, California">Daggett</a>, railroads were constructed to transport goods and people. The Southern Pacific built a line from <a href="/wiki/Mojave,_California" title="Mojave, California">Mojave, California</a> through Barstow to <a href="/wiki/Needles,_California" title="Needles, California">Needles</a> in 1883. In 1884, ownership of the line from Needles to Mojave was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Fe Railroad">Santa Fe Railroad</a>. Paving the major highways through Barstow led to further development of the city. Much of its economy depends on transportation. Before the advent of the interstate highway system, Barstow was an important stop on both Routes 66 and 91. The two routes met in downtown Barstow and continued west together to Los Angeles.</p>
<p>Barstow is named after <a href="/wiki/William_Barstow_Strong" title="William Barstow Strong">William Barstow Strong</a>, former president of the <a href="/wiki/Atchison,_Topeka_and_Santa_Fe_Railway" title="Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway">Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Some early Barstow names were Camp Sugarloaf, Grapevine, and Waterman Junction.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 107.2&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (41.4&#160;sq&#160;mi), 99.98% land and 0.02% water.<sup id="cite_ref-GR1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barstow experiences four seasons. Summer days are very hot, with highs typically exceeding 100&#160;°F (38&#160;°C). Winter, in contrast, is characterized by cold mornings, with lows near 30&#160;°F (−1&#160;°C). Daily temperature ranges are large as a result of the low atmospheric moisture, typically between 30 and 35&#160;F (16–18.5&#160;C) difference. In January, the normal high temperature is 61&#160;°F (16&#160;°C) with a low of 37&#160;°F (3&#160;°C). In July, the normal high temperature is 105&#160;°F (41&#160;°C) with a low of 74&#160;°F (23&#160;°C).<sup id="cite_ref-cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov-20">[20]</a></sup> There are an average of 140 days with highs of 90&#160;°F (32&#160;°C) or higher, an average of 82 days with highs of 100F degrees or higher, and an average of 25 days with lows of 32&#160;°F (0&#160;°C) or lower.</p>
<p>The average annual precipitation is<sup id="cite_ref-cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov-20">[20]</a></sup> 4.12 inches (10.5&#160;cm), with nearly 70% of rain typically falling during the cooler months (Nov–Apr). Snowfall is uncommon in winter, occurring every year or two.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> There are an average of 24 days annually with measurable precipitation.</p>
<p>The record high was 118&#160;°F (48&#160;°C) on July 5, 2007, and the record low was 5&#160;°F (−15&#160;°C) on December 25, 1985. The wettest year was 1918 with 10.99 inches (27.9&#160;cm) and the driest year was 1904 with 0.80 inches (2.0&#160;cm). The most rainfall in one month was 4.22 inches (10.7&#160;cm) in February 1998. The heaviest rainfall in 24&#160;hours was 2.28 inches (5.8&#160;cm) on September 10, 1976. The most snowfall in one month was 25.0 inches (64&#160;cm) in January 1949, including 7.0 inches (18&#160;cm) January 12; that month was one of the coldest and snowiest in southern California history.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov/climatenormals/clim81/CAnorm.txt">http://cdo.ncdc.noaa.gov/climatenormals/clim81/CAnorm.txt</a></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation">Vegetation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vegetation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The native vegetation is dominated by high and low <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> shrubs such as <a href="/wiki/Creosote_bush" class="mw-redirect" title="Creosote bush">creosote bush</a>. City residents have introduced many non-native plants, prominent among which are trees such as <a href="/wiki/Aleppo_pine" class="mw-redirect" title="Aleppo pine">Aleppo pine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_cypress" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian cypress">Italian cypress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fan_palm" title="Fan palm">fan palm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ailanthus" title="Ailanthus">ailanthus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ash_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Ash tree">ash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parkinsonia" title="Parkinsonia">palo verde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Redbud" class="mw-redirect" title="Redbud">redbud</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> reported that Barstow had a population of 22,639. The population density was 546.9 people per square mile (211.2/km²). The racial makeup of Barstow was 11,840 (52.3%) <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> (34.2% Non-Hispanic White),<sup id="cite_ref-quickfacts.census.gov_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quickfacts.census.gov-26">[26]</a></sup> 3,313 (14.6%) <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 477 (2.1%) <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 723 (3.2%) <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 278 (1.2%) <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 4,242 (18.7%) from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1,766 (7.8%) from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 9,700 persons (42.8%).</p>
<p>The Census reported that 22,271 people (98.4% of the population) lived in households, 195 (0.9%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 173 (0.8%) were institutionalized.</p>
<p>There were 8,085 households, out of which 3,196 (39.5%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 3,182 (39.4%) were married couples living together, 1,619 (20.0%) had a female householder with no husband present, 612 (7.6%) had a male householder with no wife present. There were 701 (8.7%) <a href="/w/index.php?title=Unmarried_partnerships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Unmarried partnerships (page does not exist)">unmarried partnerships</a>, and 58 (0.7%) <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" class="mw-redirect" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 2,174 households (26.9%) were made up of individuals and 670 (8.3%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.75. There were 5,413 <a href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (67.0% of all households); the average family size was 3.34.</p>
<p>The population was spread out with 6,739 people (29.8%) under the age of 18, 2,481 people (11.0%) aged 18 to 24, 5,723 people (25.3%) aged 25 to 44, 5,277 people (23.3%) aged 45 to 64, and 2,419 people (10.7%) who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 31.1 years. For every 100 females there were 99.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 94.9 males.</p>
<p>There were 9,555 housing units at an average density of 230.8 per square mile (89.1/km²), of which 3,964 (49.0%) were owner-occupied, and 4,121 (51.0%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 5.0%; the rental vacancy rate was 16.0%. 10,829 people (47.8% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 11,442 people (50.5%) lived in rental housing units.</p>
<p>During 2009–2013, Barstow had a median household income of $42,354, with 26.2% of the population living below the federal poverty line.<sup id="cite_ref-quickfacts.census.gov_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quickfacts.census.gov-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the census<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-27">[27]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 21,119 people, 7,647 households, and 5,253 families residing in the city. The population density was 628.8 inhabitants per square mile (242.8/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 9,153 housing units at an average density of 272.5 per square mile (105.2/km<sup>2</sup>).</p>
<p>The racial makeup of the city was 57.1% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 11.6% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 2.4% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 3.1% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 1.0% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 18.4% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 6.5% from two or more races. 36.5% of the population were <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> or Latin American of any race.</p>
<p>There were 7,647 households out of which 36.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 45.4% were married couples living together, 17.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 31.3% were non-families. 25.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.7 and the average family size was 3.3.</p>
<p>In the city, the population was spread out with 30.8% under the age of 18, 10.4% from 18 to 24, 27.4% from 25 to 44, 19.3% from 45 to 64, and 12.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females there were 99.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 97.1 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $35,069, and the median income for a family was $40,160. Males had a median income of $37,425 versus $25,380 for females. The per capita income for the city was $16,132. About 15.6% of families and 20.3% of the population were below the poverty line, including 30.8% of those under age 18 and 4.3% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<p>Barstow has a series of <a href="/wiki/Mural" title="Mural">murals</a> along Main Street, depicting scenes from the city's history. These murals were created by Main Street Murals, a local non-profit organization.</p>
<p>Barstow Branch Library is located at 304 E. Buena Vista Street. It is a community venue, running various activities such as a summer reading program for children, story and craft sessions as well as a mystery book club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skyline Drive-In, a <a href="/wiki/Drive-in_theater" title="Drive-in theater">drive-in theater</a> located in the north-east outskirts of the city at 31175 Old Highway 58, is one of the last operating in San Bernardino County. It has two screens; each screen shows two movies every night.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hollywood Theatre Barstow Cinema 6 is the city's indoor cinema. It has six screens and can be found at 1503 East Main Street, in the east side of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> As of September 30, 2011, Skyline Drive-In took over Hollywood Theatre, changing its name back to Barstow Station Cinema.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barstow Community College has a $22 million Performing Arts Center which hosts college theatre and music performances, and traveling professional performances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Barstow has a number of museums: Mojave River Valley Museum, Route 66 Mother Road Museum, the Western America Rail Museum, and the Desert Discovery Center. Once a year a family opens their Black History collection to the public and nearby Fort Irwin is home to the 11 Cavalry and ACR Museum. The <a href="/wiki/Old_Woman_meteorite" title="Old Woman meteorite">Old Woman meteorite</a>, the largest meteorite found in California and the second largest in the United States, is housed in the Desert Discovery Center.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Harvey_House_(Barstow,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvey House (Barstow, California)">Casa Del Desierto</a>, built in 1911 as a <a href="/wiki/Fred_Harvey_Company" title="Fred Harvey Company">Harvey House</a> hotel and train station, now houses the Route 66 Mother Road Museum, the <a href="/wiki/Western_America_Railroad_Museum" title="Western America Railroad Museum">Western America Railroad Museum</a> and still functions as an (unstaffed) <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> station. The Barstow Chamber of Commerce sponsors an annual sandcastle contest in the dry riverbed across from the Harvey House.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Restaurants">Restaurants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Restaurants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Opened in 1975 and operating 365 days a year, Barstow Station serves 20,000 tour buses a year and is a popular stop for travelers on <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15" title="Interstate 15">Interstate 15</a>. The site includes a number of gift shops, an ice cream parlour, a <a href="/wiki/Panda_Express" title="Panda Express">Panda Express</a>, <a href="/wiki/Popeyes_Louisiana_Kitchen" title="Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen">Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen</a>, KHWY radio station and a <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound</a> ticket terminal. In November 2013, <a href="/wiki/Dunkin%27_Donuts" title="Dunkin' Donuts">Dunkin' Donuts</a> opened inside Barstow Station, becoming only the second location of that chain within the entire state of California (following a shop on Camp Pendleton) and the first to be accessible to the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a> restaurant at Barstow Station consists of three side-by-side railroad cars—a reference to Barstow's railroad heritage. In September 1986, the restaurant was destroyed by fire when a customer's car burst into flames at the drive-up window.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In June 1997, the re-built restaurant received national attention when a gunman opened fire during a botched robbery, injuring several people and killing a nine-year-old girl. The gunman was mortally wounded by an off-duty police officer after the ensuing gun battle and later died in hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other casual dining options include: <a href="/wiki/In-N-Out_Burger" title="In-N-Out Burger">In-N-Out Burger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>, <a href="/wiki/IHOP" title="IHOP">IHOP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denny%27s" title="Denny's">Denny's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_in_the_Box" title="Jack in the Box">Jack in the Box</a>. The oldest still operating franchise of <a href="/wiki/Del_Taco" title="Del Taco">Del Taco</a> restaurant can be found at 401 N. First Street. Other privately owned restaurants provide Italian, American and Mexican cuisine.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Located southwest of the town is the upscale Tanger Center Barstow, of <a href="/wiki/Tanger_Factory_Outlet_Centers" title="Tanger Factory Outlet Centers">Tanger Factory Outlet Centers</a> chain, which is a popular stop for tourists traveling between Greater Los Angeles and Las Vegas.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> An older shopping center of <a href="/wiki/Outlet_store" title="Outlet store">outlet stores</a>, the Barstow Factory Outlet, is located opposite the Tanger Outlet Center.</p>
<p>The town has an enclosed shopping mall, <a href="/wiki/Barstow_Mall" title="Barstow Mall">Barstow Mall</a>, built in the 1970s. It was renovated in 2010 and is attracting new tenants, including the County of San Bernardino's new social service office for the Transition Assistance Department and Children and Family Services.</p>
<p>Barstow also has branches of <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vons" title="Vons">Vons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stater_Bros." title="Stater Bros.">Stater Bros.</a>, <a href="/wiki/99_Cents_Only_Stores" title="99 Cents Only Stores">99 Cents Only Stores</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" title="The Home Depot">The Home Depot</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hardware" title="Ace Hardware">Ace Hardware</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Near_Barstow">Near Barstow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Near Barstow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>'s National Training Center (NTC) and <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Goldstone_Deep_Space_Communications_Complex" title="Goldstone Deep Space Communications Complex">Goldstone Deep Space Communications Complex</a> are located at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Fort_Irwin_National_Training_Center" title="Fort Irwin National Training Center">Fort Irwin</a>, north of Barstow. The Goldstone Complex includes the <a href="/wiki/Deep_Space_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Deep Space Network">Pioneer Deep Space Station</a>, which has been designated a U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">National Historic Landmark</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Calico,_San_Bernardino_County,_California" title="Calico, San Bernardino County, California">Calico Ghost Town</a> is one of the few remaining original mining towns of the western United States, now preserved as a museum by <a href="/wiki/Walter_Knott" title="Walter Knott">Walter Knott</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Basin" title="Rainbow Basin">Rainbow Basin</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Critical_Environmental_Concern" title="Area of Critical Environmental Concern">Area of Critical Environmental Concern</a> due to landscape features and paleontological resources in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Located 8 miles (13&#160;km) north of Barstow, its diverse landscape, multi-colored rock formations and scenic canyons are popular with photographers, hikers and campers keen to experience the area's natural beauty. The <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossiliferous</a> <a href="/wiki/Barstow_Formation" title="Barstow Formation">Barstow Formation</a> (<a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a>) is well exposed there. Rainbow Basin is managed by the <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Land_Management" title="Bureau of Land Management">Bureau of Land Management</a>'s Barstow Field Office.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Coyote_Lake_(San_Bernardino_County,_California)" title="Coyote Lake (San Bernardino County, California)">Coyote Dry Lake</a> is a 30 square miles (78&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Dry_lake" title="Dry lake">dry lake</a> located 15 miles (24&#160;km) northeast of Barstow.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lava_tube" title="Lava tube">Lava tubes</a> offer <a href="/wiki/Caving" title="Caving">spelunking</a> opportunities.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Solar_Project" title="The Solar Project">The Solar Project</a> is located in Daggett, CA, about 10&#160;miles (16&#160;km) east of Barstow.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barstow is home to the Barstow Riffians, a developmental semi-professional football team. The Barstow Riffians are members of the UFAL.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city offers adult basketball and softball leagues.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barstow Community College's athletics department offers four competitive intercollegiate sports programs: Men's and Women's Basketball, Men's and Women's Baseball and Softball.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks_and_recreation">Parks and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Parks and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barstow has two main parks: the Barstow Skate Park,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> a 12,000 square-foot skate park, and the Robert A. Sessions Memorial Sportspark,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> which includes six lighted ball fields, three soccer fields, volleyball courts, batting cages as well as basketball courts. The Robert A. Sessions Memorial Sportspark also plays host to regional softball tournaments.</p>
<p>The city also has the Dana Park Community Center open on weekdays, the Cora Harper Fitness Center and Tennis Courts open Monday to Saturday, and the outdoor Eda Henderson Pool open Tuesdays to Sundays throughout the school summer holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Founded in the 1970s by two local residents, the thriving Barstow Senior Center serves Barstow's seniors. In addition to daily, weekly and monthly activities, there is also an onsite thrift store and lunches are served every weekday. The center is funded via annual membership fees and sponsorship.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_and_federal_representation">State and federal representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: State and federal representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">California State Legislature</a>, Barstow is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_16th_State_Senate_district" title="California's 16th State Senate district">the 16th Senate District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Republican_Party" title="California Republican Party">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Fuller" title="Jean Fuller">Jean Fuller</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_33rd_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 33rd State Assembly district">the 33rd Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Republican_Party" title="California Republican Party">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Jay_Obernolte" title="Jay Obernolte">Jay Obernolte</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a>, Barstow is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_8th_congressional_district" title="California's 8th congressional district">California's 8th congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cook_(politician)" title="Paul Cook (politician)">Paul Cook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its long distance from major cities and urban centers has created economic problems, and Barstow is seeking projects to boost the economy. However, there are three casinos planned for the area. Numerous construction projects have been announced for Barstow, including a Wal-Mart distribution center, retail growth, and an increase in lodging accommodation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barstow Unified School District has 8 elementary schools (grades 1–6):</p>
<ul>
<li>Cameron Elementary School</li>
<li>Crestline Elementary School</li>
<li>Henderson Elementary School</li>
<li>Hinkley Elementary School (Hinkley, California. Now Closed)</li>
<li>Lenwood Elementary School</li>
<li>Montara Elementary School</li>
<li>Skyline North Elementary School</li>
<li>Thomson Elementary School (Now Barstow Stem Academy)</li>
</ul>
<p>Grades 7–8 are taught at Barstow Junior High School and grades 9–12 are taught at <a href="/wiki/Barstow_High_School" title="Barstow High School">Barstow High School</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Barstow_Community_College" title="Barstow Community College">Barstow Community College</a> is the only college in the area, and its primary objectives are to enable students to transfer to a four-year college or university and learn vocational trades through Career Technical Education.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Park_University" title="Park University">Park University</a> has a campus located at the Marine Corps Logistics Base, which accepts local civilian students as well as military personnel and their dependents.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Park also offers classes on the community college campus.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newspaper.2C_books.2C_and_radio">Newspaper, books, and radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Newspaper, books, and radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Founded in 1910 and renamed in 1958, the <a href="/wiki/Desert_Dispatch" title="Desert Dispatch">Desert Dispatch</a> is Barstow's local newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The newspaper has a daily circulation of 3,259<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and was awarded second place for Sports Page Design, Opinion Page Design and Editorial Comment at the 2008 Better Newspapers Contest, hosted by the <a href="/wiki/California_Newspaper_Publishers_Association" title="California Newspaper Publishers Association">California Newspaper Publishers Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barstow's main radio station is <a href="/wiki/KDUC" title="KDUC">KDUC</a> (or "K-DUCK"), which plays adult contemporary music and also serves <a href="/wiki/Victorville,_California" title="Victorville, California">Victorville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Valley,_California" title="Apple Valley, California">Apple Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hesperia,_California" title="Hesperia, California">Hesperia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ridgecrest,_California" title="Ridgecrest, California">Ridgecrest, California</a>.</p>
<p>"We were somewhere around Barstow on the edge of the desert when the drugs began to take hold." is the first line in <a href="/wiki/Hunter_S._Thompson" title="Hunter S. Thompson">Hunter S. Thompson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_and_Loathing_in_Las_Vegas" title="Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas">Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Movies_and_television">Movies and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Movies and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barstow City Council has a dedicated film office, which acts as a point of liaison and resources for film locations, equipment and accommodation for filmmakers and their crews.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> A number of notable motion pictures were shot in the city,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> including <i><a href="/wiki/Broken_Arrow_(1996_film)" title="Broken Arrow (1996 film)">Broken Arrow</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Courage_Under_Fire" title="Courage Under Fire">Courage Under Fire</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/From_Dusk_till_Dawn" title="From Dusk till Dawn">From Dusk till Dawn</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Gattaca" title="Gattaca">Gattaca</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Erin_Brockovich_(film)" title="Erin Brockovich (film)">Erin Brockovich</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_Bill:_Volume_2" title="Kill Bill: Volume 2">Kill Bill: Volume 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Other notable mentions of Barstow include the 2008 movie <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leaving_Barstow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leaving Barstow (page does not exist)">Leaving Barstow</a></i>, which tells the story of a high school senior who must choose between his ambitions to leave Barstow or stay in the city to care for his mother. The fictional <a href="/wiki/Brian_O%27Conner" title="Brian O'Conner">Brian O'Conner</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/2_Fast_2_Furious" title="2 Fast 2 Furious">2 Fast 2 Furious</a></i> grew up in Barstow and travels to the city to persuade a former childhood friend to join him in an <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">FBI</a> operation.</p>
<p>The film <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_and_Loathing_in_Las_Vegas_(film)" title="Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas (film)">Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas</a></i> mentions Barstow frequently. Barstow is also mentioned in 2009 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hangover" title="The Hangover">The Hangover</a></i> before the roadtrip from Los Angeles to Las Vegas. The <a href="/wiki/Hair_(film)" title="Hair (film)">movie version</a> of "Hair" was partially filmed in Barstow in the late 1970s.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2015_in_film" title="2015 in film">2015</a>, Barstow was one of the filming locations for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_(film)" title="Sky (film)">Sky</a></i> as well as <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Beach,_California" title="Bombay Beach, California">Bombay Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hinkley,_California" title="Hinkley, California">Hinkley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Tree,_California" title="Joshua Tree, California">Joshua Tree</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landers,_California" title="Landers, California">Landers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lenwood,_California" title="Lenwood, California">Lenwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludlow,_California" title="Ludlow, California">Ludlow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newberry_Springs,_California" title="Newberry Springs, California">Newberry Springs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Victorville,_California" title="Victorville, California">Victorville, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IMDb_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb-61">[61]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:Public_transportation_in_San_Bernardino_County,_California" title="Category:Public transportation in San Bernardino County, California">Category:Public transportation in San Bernardino County, California</a></div>
<p>Barstow Area Transit (BAT) is the local transportation system.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> It covers the city of Barstow and the surrounding areas in San Bernardino County. BAT operates three fixed city bus routes on an hourly schedule, a dial-a-ride service for seniors and persons with disabilities and two county routes serving <a href="/wiki/Hinkley,_California" title="Hinkley, California">Hinkley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yermo,_California" title="Yermo, California">Yermo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daggett,_California" title="Daggett, California">Daggett</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Newberry_Springs,_California" title="Newberry Springs, California">Newberry Springs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The county services operate on a fixed route with a <i>deviation zone</i> and a flexible time schedule. When requested in advance, the county bus travels off the fixed route to pick up or drop off passengers within the deviation zone. Unless passengers hold a monthly or day pass, there is an additional charge for this service. All city and county buses connect at Barstow City Hall Transport Center. Intercity buses that serve Barstow include <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_Belt_Stages" title="Orange Belt Stages">Orange Belt Stages</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_California_cities_with_Aerom%C3%A9xico_bus_service_from_Tijuana_airport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="List of California cities with Aeroméxico bus service from Tijuana airport (page does not exist)">Intercalifornias</a>, <a href="/wiki/TUFESA" title="TUFESA">TUFESA</a>, and Fronteras del Norte.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Harvey_House_(Barstow,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Harvey House (Barstow, California)">Harvey House Railroad Depot</a> is served twice daily by <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Chief" title="Southwest Chief">Southwest Chief</a>, from Chicago to Los Angeles and reverse. Connections can also be made on multiple Amtrak <a href="/wiki/Thruway_Motorcoach" class="mw-redirect" title="Thruway Motorcoach">Thruway Motorcoach</a> bus services to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a> and other destinations. Rail freight is provided by the <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Barstow-Daggett_Airport" title="Barstow-Daggett Airport">Barstow-Daggett Airport</a> is the local airport that serves <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> but has no commercial service.</p>
<p>Roads are the main method of transport. The primary arteries serving Barstow are <a href="/wiki/Interstate_40_in_California" title="Interstate 40 in California">Interstate 40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_in_California" title="Interstate 15 in California">Interstate 15</a>, and <a href="/wiki/California_State_Route_58" title="California State Route 58">California State Route 58</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Barstow_Community_Hospital" title="Barstow Community Hospital">Barstow Community Hospital</a> is a 56-bed hospital serving the surrounding High Desert community. Opened in 1958, the hospital was named one of the "100 Top Hospitals in the Nation" for two consecutive years in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_safety">Public safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Public safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barstow has its own police department, plus a regional station of the <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino_County_Sheriff%27s_Department" title="San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department">San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department</a>, which serves the unincorporated areas around the city, including <a href="/wiki/Newberry_Springs,_California" title="Newberry Springs, California">Newberry Springs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trona,_San_Bernardino_County,_California" title="Trona, San Bernardino County, California">Trona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baker,_California" title="Baker, California">Baker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludlow,_California" title="Ludlow, California">Ludlow</a>.</p>
<p>Fire prevention and paramedic services are provided by the Barstow Fire Protection District.</p>
<p>On November 19, 2010 Barstow residents were warned that the area water system was contaminated.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> A local area military base, <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Logistics_Base_Barstow" title="Marine Corps Logistics Base Barstow">Marine Corps Logistics Base Barstow</a> notified Golden State Water that samples taken from their water system showed the chemical <a href="/wiki/Perchlorate" title="Perchlorate">perchlorate</a> at levels above the maximum contaminant level of 6 parts per billion. Perchlorate is found in rocket propellant, fireworks, explosives, flares, matches and industrial byproducts. Golden State Water Co. handed out free bottled water to residents. The "Do Not Drink" water advisory ended five days later on November 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cemetery">Cemetery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barstow,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Cemetery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
