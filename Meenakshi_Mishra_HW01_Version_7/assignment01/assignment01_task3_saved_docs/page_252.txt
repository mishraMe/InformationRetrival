<p>The <b>Tesla Gigafactory 1</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Lithium-ion_battery" title="Lithium-ion battery">lithium-ion battery</a> factory under construction, primarily for <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Inc." title="Tesla Inc.">Tesla Inc.</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Tahoe_Reno_Industrial_Center" title="Tahoe Reno Industrial Center">Tahoe Reno Industrial Center</a> (TRIC) in <a href="/wiki/Storey_County,_Nevada" title="Storey County, Nevada">Storey County</a> (near the Community of <a href="/wiki/Clark,_Nevada" title="Clark, Nevada">Clark, Nevada</a>, US).<sup id="cite_ref-TeslaEnergyPresentation1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TeslaEnergyPresentation1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tric1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tric1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytWin_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytWin-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The factory started limited production of <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Powerwall" title="Tesla Powerwall">Powerwalls and Powerpacks</a> in the first quarter of 2016<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ct2016-01-08_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ct2016-01-08-2">[2]</a></sup> using battery <a href="/wiki/Electrochemical_cell" title="Electrochemical cell">cells</a> produced elsewhere, and began <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a> of cells in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-bloo2017-01-04_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloo2017-01-04-7">[7]</a></sup> Nevada Governor <a href="/wiki/Brian_Sandoval" title="Brian Sandoval">Brian Sandoval</a> estimated that Nevada would enjoy $100 billion in economic benefit over two decades from the construction and operation of this factory.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg20140905_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg20140905-8">[8]</a></sup> The grand opening event was held on July 29, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second factory—<a href="/wiki/Gigafactory_2" title="Gigafactory 2">Gigafactory 2</a>—is slated to be built in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (location to be announced in 2017), as of news reports in November 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initial public mention of the gigafactory concept was made in November 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-mercurynews20141115_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mercurynews20141115-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> although Tesla's internal plans predated that; Tesla had investigated almost 100 sites.</p>
<p>In July 2014, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a> had reached a basic agreement with Tesla to invest in a factory,<sup id="cite_ref-zdnet20141007_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zdnet20141007-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> estimated to cost $5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teslaGigafactory201402_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGigafactory201402-17">[17]</a></sup> The TRIC owners gave the first 1,000 acres to Tesla for free.<sup id="cite_ref-lvsun1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lvsun1-18">[18]</a></sup> The Reno site and plans were announced with Nevada officials on September 3, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-nytWin_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytWin-5">[5]</a></sup> Panasonic will lead the battery cell production portion of the manufacturing, and Tesla CEO <a href="/wiki/Elon_Musk" title="Elon Musk">Elon Musk</a> indicated in 2015 that the total Panasonic investment would be <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$1.5–2 billion</span>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and that Tesla would not expand beyond original plans.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In early 2016 Panasonic president <a href="/wiki/Kazuhiro_Tsuga" title="Kazuhiro Tsuga">Kazuhiro Tsuga</a> confirmed a planned total investment of about $1.6 billion by the company to equip the factory to full capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> However, after <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Model_3#Market" title="Tesla Model 3">the number of Model 3 reservations</a> became known in April,<sup id="cite_ref-verge2016-07-28_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verge2016-07-28-22">[22]</a></sup> Panasonic moved production plans forward<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and announced a <a href="/wiki/Bond_(finance)" title="Bond (finance)">bond</a> sale for $3.86 billion, most of it to be invested in Gigafactory.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tesla held a <a href="/wiki/Grand_opening" title="Grand opening">grand opening</a> on July 29, 2016 of the operational facility, having only three of the final 21 "blocks" of the gigafactory built out, or approximately 14 percent of the final factory size expected by 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-elektrek20160728_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elektrek20160728-28">[28]</a></sup> By September 2016 Tesla had spent $608 million on Gigafactory.<sup id="cite_ref-sec2016-09_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec2016-09-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2016, it was announced that Tesla would build motors and drive units at the Gigafactory, in addition to the previously announced batteries and assembled battery packs called Powerpacks.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rationale">Rationale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rationale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Tesla expects that Gigafactory 1 will reduce the <a href="/wiki/Production_cost" class="mw-redirect" title="Production cost">production cost</a> for their <a href="/wiki/Electric_vehicle_battery" title="Electric vehicle battery">electric vehicle battery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Powerwall" title="Tesla Powerwall">Powerwall and Powerpack</a> <a href="/wiki/Battery_pack" title="Battery pack">packs</a> by 30%. Its projected capacity for 2018 is 50&#160;GWh/yr of battery packs and its final capacity upon completion of entire factory is 150&#160;GWh/yr. This would enable Tesla to produce 1,500,000 cars per year.<sup id="cite_ref-teslaGrandOpening20160730_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGrandOpening20160730-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary.2C_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (August 2016)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-teslaGigafactory201402_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGigafactory201402-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2016)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The factory is designed to optimize quality while minimizing cost of production and raw material utilization through <a href="/wiki/Vertical_integration" title="Vertical integration">vertical integration</a>. Tesla expects to achieve a cost target for production battery packs of under <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$100</span> per <a href="/wiki/KWh" class="mw-redirect" title="KWh">kWh</a> of <a href="/wiki/Energy_storage" title="Energy storage">energy storage</a> by 2020, near the "<a href="/wiki/Inflection_point" title="Inflection point">inflection point</a> at which it begins [to cost] less to build an electric powertrain than [an <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">internal combustion engine</a> powertrain] even without subsidies or [considering] the savings of <a href="/wiki/Electrical_energy" title="Electrical energy">electricity</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gas</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-elektrek20160728_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elektrek20160728-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gigafactory will produce batteries for significantly less cost using economies of scale, innovative manufacturing, reduction of waste, and the simple optimization of locating most manufacturing process under one roof.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>Tesla initially considered several sites, but Reno was not one of them. A manager at <a href="/wiki/Reno%E2%80%93Tahoe_International_Airport" title="Reno–Tahoe International Airport">Reno–Tahoe International Airport</a> offered some of its 3,000 available acres at <a href="/wiki/Reno_Stead_Airport" title="Reno Stead Airport">Reno Stead Airport</a>, and pursuaded TRIC owner to split the bill for a <a href="/wiki/Private_jet" class="mw-redirect" title="Private jet">private jet</a> to fly Tesla people to Reno. Reno tried several times, and Tesla eventually came to TRIC and was pleased by the speed of <a href="/wiki/Construction_permit" title="Construction permit">regulatory work</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Iftiger_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iftiger-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="State_competition_and_incentives">State competition and incentives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: State competition and incentives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>At least five <a href="/wiki/Tax_competition" title="Tax competition">states competed</a> to attract Gigafactory by offering tax incentives, cash grants<sup id="cite_ref-gtm2014_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gtm2014-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jalop2014_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jalop2014-40">[40]</a></sup> and other methods in the hope of future business; <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-art_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-art-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tx_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tx-45">[45]</a></sup> where <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a> had offered bigger incentives with no sales tax, but did not get the factory. States without sales tax were topping the list of preferred sites.<sup id="cite_ref-rgjBillion_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgjBillion-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-art_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-art-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-boyd_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boyd-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kirkland1_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kirkland1-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ralston_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ralston-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nevada estimates a <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">tax</a> base of $1.9 billion over 20 years.<sup id="cite_ref-DNsummary_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DNsummary-35">[35]</a></sup> After sticky negotiations,<sup id="cite_ref-ralston_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ralston-48">[48]</a></sup> Tesla chose the Tahoe-Reno Industrial Center (TRIC) as the location of the Gigafactory mainly due to speed<sup id="cite_ref-art_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-art-44">[44]</a></sup> and a State of Nevada incentive package. These incentives include $195 million in transferable tax credits depending on Tesla's investment schedule and job creation, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Chattanooga_Assembly_Plant" title="Volkswagen Chattanooga Assembly Plant">Volkswagen Chattanooga Assembly Plant</a> deal and others.<sup id="cite_ref-rgjBillion_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgjBillion-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kirkland1_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kirkland1-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ralston_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ralston-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-elecB3_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elecB3-49">[49]</a></sup> Tesla's investments earn them about $10 million in tax credits per quarter,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> and by July 2016 had sold these for $20 million cash.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Other location reasons were rail access, <a href="/wiki/Tesla_US_dealership_disputes" title="Tesla US dealership disputes">direct sale ability</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-jalop2014_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jalop2014-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-boyd_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boyd-46">[46]</a></sup> and low air <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fc2015_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fc2015-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>The incentive package also includes 20 years free from <a href="/wiki/Sales_taxes_in_the_United_States#Nevada" title="Sales taxes in the United States">sales tax</a> and 10 years free from <a href="/wiki/Property_tax_in_the_United_States" title="Property tax in the United States">property tax</a>, depending on Tesla's ability to meet performance expectations (like investing $3.5 billion in Nevada).<sup id="cite_ref-rgjBillion_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgjBillion-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ralston_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ralston-48">[48]</a></sup> By 2034, this package could have accumulated to a value of $1.25 billion in missed taxes; the <a href="/wiki/Tax_incentive#List_of_largest_US_tax_incentive_deals" title="Tax incentive">10th largest in the US</a>. The $725M sales tax abatement was particularly important, as 5 other states charge no sales tax at all and 34 states, including Arizona and Texas, don't charge sales tax on manufacturing equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-rgjBillion_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgjBillion-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kirkland1_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kirkland1-47">[47]</a></sup> With a tax base of $1.9 billion and an incentive package of $1.25 billion, the projected end result was calculated as a <a href="/wiki/Tax_holiday" title="Tax holiday">tax-per-abatement</a> ratio of 1.52.<sup id="cite_ref-nvIncentive_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvIncentive-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-art_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-art-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The nearby <a href="/wiki/Data_center" title="Data center">data centers</a> from <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switch_(company)" title="Switch (company)">Switch</a> also received incentives.<sup id="cite_ref-art_44-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-art-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nevada estimates the construction impact at $2.4 billion and the economic impact from the project at $100 billion over two decades ($5 billion/year, of which $353 million are <a href="/wiki/Wage" title="Wage">wages</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-nvIncentive_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvIncentive-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DNsummary_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DNsummary-35">[35]</a></sup> Some economists said that number was "deeply flawed", for instance, it counted every Tesla employee as if they would otherwise have been unemployed and made no allowance for increased government spending to serve the influx of thousands of local residents.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg20140905_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg20140905-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-taxrebate_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taxrebate-58">[58]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-taxrebate1_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taxrebate1-59">[59]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-taxrebate2_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taxrebate2-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rtRebate_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rtRebate-61">[61]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-b49_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-b49-62">[62]</a></sup> Tesla agreed to pay $7.5M per year for 5 years ($37.5M) to the <a href="/wiki/K%E2%80%9312" title="K–12">school system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rgjBillion_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgjBillion-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Logistics">Logistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Logistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

Since 2004, the TRIC owners had planned to extend SR 439 south to <img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/US_50.svg/20px-US_50.svg.png" width="20" height="20" Route 50 in Nevada">U.S. Route 50</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Tesla plans to ship batteries by <a href="/wiki/Rail_freight_transport" title="Rail <p><a href="/wiki/Water_scarcity" title="Water scarcity">Water is scarce</a> in Nevada, and some of the water for the Gigafactory is piped from a <a href="/wiki/Water_treatment" title="Water treatment">treatment plant</a> in the neighboring Washoe County.<sup id="cite_ref-ralston_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ralston-48">[48]</a></sup> A 1.5 million U.S. gallons (5.7 million L) water tank is also used (about two <a href="/wiki/Olympic-size_swimming_pool" title="Olympic-size swimming pool">Olympic-size swimming pools</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-buildzoom_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buildzoom-72">[72]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-buildzoom2_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buildzoom2-73">[73]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>TRIC built high-pressure <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas line">natural gas lines</a> to its sites, but Tesla decided to use electricity and not connect natural gas to Gigafactory.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heat_pump" title="Heat pump">Heat pump</a> technology is used for heating,<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> and a $6.8 million <a href="/wiki/Hot_water_storage_tank" title="Hot water storage tank">energy storage tank</a> is scheduled for the site.<sup id="cite_ref-buildzoom_72-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buildzoom-72">[72]</a></sup> In 2014 <a href="/wiki/Navigant_Consulting" title="Navigant Consulting">Navigant</a> estimated 100 MW <a href="/wiki/Electricity_delivery" title="Electricity delivery">electricity delivery</a> which could be supplied (on average) by nearby <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbines" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind turbines">wind turbines</a> and roof solar panels,<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> whereas a former Tesla <a href="/wiki/Logistics" title="Logistics">logistics</a> manager in 2016 estimated 300 MW to produce 35 GWh of battery capacity per year.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tesla works with a mining company to <a href="/wiki/Brine_mining" title="Brine mining">extract lithium</a> 200 miles (320&#160;km) to the southeast, at <a href="/wiki/Silver_Peak,_Nevada" title="Silver Peak, Nevada">Silver Peak</a> in <a href="/wiki/Esmeralda_County,_Nevada" title="Esmeralda County, Nevada">Esmeralda County</a>. They intend to process the underground <a href="/wiki/Brine" title="Brine">brine</a> water industrially over hours rather than the traditional way of letting the water evaporate from ponds over a year.<sup id="cite_ref-tx_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tx-45">[45]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>

By September 2016, the building had grown to 1.9 million sq&#160;ft (0.2 million m<sup>2</sup>) with further areas under construction.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup>The current square footage of the factory is 5.5 million sq ft. A combined permit value of $322,568,793 was registered in July 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> and $477m in November 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-buildzoom_72-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buildzoom-72">[72]</a></sup> Gigafactory and the other large companies affect the local area by increased needs in transportation (SR 439) and <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Nevada" title="Dayton, Nevada">housing</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> house prices increased by 17% in 2015 from half of the peak in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_housing_bubble" title="United States housing bubble">previous 2008 building bubble</a>. Local businesses were wary of building new homes, which is being done mostly by out-of-state companies.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/HVAC" title="HVAC">HVAC</a> equipment is located "between the floors rather than on walls and ceilings of a floor."<sup id="cite_ref-elektrek20160728_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elektrek20160728-28">[28]</a></sup> All steel is from the US. Tesla is its own <a href="/wiki/General_contractor" title="General contractor">contractor</a> on the project, learning how to build other factories should they choose so.<sup id="cite_ref-verge2016-07-28_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verge2016-07-28-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-buildzoom2_73-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buildzoom2-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_expansion">Future expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Future expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the building of more gigafactories (see below), Tesla may expand and potentially double the size of Gigafactory 1. In June 2015, Tesla announced it exercised its option to buy 1,864 acres (754 hectares) of land adjacent to the original 1,000-acre (400&#160;ha) Gigafactory site. Tesla spokeswoman Alexis Georgeson. "The purchase gives us the opportunity for future growth." In Tesla's dealings with the state of Nevada expanding the size was always an option should the company choose to do so, said Steve Hill, director of the Governor's Office of Economic Development. "Tesla had said that the factory will be up to 10 million square feet [1 million square meters] in one or two stories," Hill said. "On the earnings call (in May 2015), Elon said they aren't yet committing to this but that they are considering increasing the size of the gigafactory here by 50 to 100 percent." <sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early estimates from 2014 projected that the factory would employ approximately 6,500 people by 2022, requiring at least half of them to be Nevadans.<sup id="cite_ref-nvIncentive_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvIncentive-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tesla owns the land and building, and leases part of the building to Panasonic, which owns some of the cell production equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-sec2016-10-11_92-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec2016-10-11-92">[92]</a></sup> The gigafactory is operated by a management team under executive <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jens_Peter_Clausen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jens Peter Clausen (page does not exist)">Jens Peter Clausen</a>, formerly a <a href="/wiki/LEGO" class="mw-redirect" title="LEGO">LEGO</a> executive, who is VP of Gigafactory at Tesla.<sup id="cite_ref-electrek20160309_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electrek20160309-93">[93]</a></sup> By April 2016 Gigafactory had 317 Tesla employees and 52 by Panasonic, most of them from Nevada,<sup id="cite_ref-elecWork_85-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elecWork-85">[85]</a></sup> and 850 by December 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-electrek20161209_94-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electrek20161209-94">[94]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The factory is intended to gradually<sup id="cite_ref-qz745278_95-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qz745278-95">[95]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tesla2017-01-04_96-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tesla2017-01-04-96">[96]</a></sup> provide <a href="/wiki/Cradle-to-cradle_design" title="Cradle-to-cradle design">cradle-to-cradle</a> handling of batteries, from <a href="/wiki/Raw_material" title="Raw material">raw material</a> over components to finished products, and <a href="/wiki/Battery_recycling#Lithium_ion_batteries" title="Battery recycling">recycling</a> old batteries into new.<sup id="cite_ref-teslaGigafactory201402_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGigafactory201402-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Electrochemical_cell" title="Electrochemical cell">Cells</a> constitute most of the value creation, whereas packing and electronics are minor parts.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> Tesla views <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing_engineering" title="Manufacturing engineering">production</a> as more important than products, and assigns more <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineers</a> to <a href="/wiki/Production_engineering" title="Production engineering">developing production equipment</a> than to developing products.<sup id="cite_ref-teslaGroh_98-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGroh-98">[98]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cava2016-07-26_99-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cava2016-07-26-99">[99]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fehr2016-06-06_100-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fehr2016-06-06-100">[100]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cell_level">Cell level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cell level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The basis of the energy storage system of Tesla products are lithium-ion cells in the 18650 form factor. These cylindrical cells have a diameter of 18&#160;mm and are 65&#160;mm in length, a size used for the batteries of laptops. Cylindrical cells are generally less expensive (costing 190–200 dollars per kWh as of 2014) than large format cells whose active layers are stacked or folded (approximately 240–250 dollars per kWh).<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup></p>
<p>The battery cells produced at the Gigafactory are of a new form factor, larger than the 18650 cells used in the Model S and Model X automobiles. While the cells were originally expected to be at least 20&#160;mm in diameter and 70&#160;mm in length,<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> revised specifications for the optimized form factor are 21&#160;mm (0.83&#160;in) by 70&#160;mm (2.8&#160;in). Tesla thus refers to it as the '<a href="/wiki/21700" class="mw-redirect" title="21700">21-70</a>',<sup id="cite_ref-elektrek20160728_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elektrek20160728-28">[28]</a></sup> whereas <a href="/wiki/Samsung#Samsung_SDI" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> refer to the size as '21700'.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> Panasonic is expected to begin cell production in 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-electrek20160309_93-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electrek20160309-93">[93]</a></sup> and continue for at least 10 years.<sup id="cite_ref-sec2016-10-11_92-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec2016-10-11-92">[92]</a></sup> Only Panasonic cells are to be used in the Model 3.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> Among the machines spotted at the opening in July 2016 were <a href="/wiki/Machine_press" title="Machine press">presses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolling_(metalworking)" title="Rolling (metalworking)">rollers</a> for <a href="/wiki/Cathode" title="Cathode">cathodes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> Gigafactory began <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a> of the 2170 in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-bloo2017-01-04_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloo2017-01-04-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tesla2017-01-04_96-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tesla2017-01-04-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_level">Product level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Product level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In October 2015, Tesla moved the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Powerwall" title="Tesla Powerwall">Tesla Powerwall</a> and Powerpack production from its Fremont factory to the Gigafactory. The Tesla Powerwall has been produced in the finished portion since the third quarter of 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-rj2016-03-18_84-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rj2016-03-18-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Output_goals">Output goals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Output goals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the projected capacity of Gigafactory for 2020 was to have been 35 gigawatt-hours per year of cells as well as 50&#160;GWh/yr of battery packs.<sup id="cite_ref-zdnet20141007_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zdnet20141007-13">[13]</a></sup> Production could be equivalent of supplying 500,000 Tesla cars per year.<sup id="cite_ref-teslaGigafactory201402_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslaGigafactory201402-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teslamotors_83-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teslamotors-83">[83]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ibt20140320_107-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ibt20140320-107">[107]</a></sup> When finished, the factory is planned to produce more lithium ion batteries in a year than were produced in the entire world in 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_Gigafactories">Future Gigafactories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Future Gigafactories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gigafactory_2" title="Gigafactory 2">Gigafactory 2</a></div>
<p>On April 30, 2015, Elon Musk announced that the factory heretofore known simply as the <i>Gigafactory</i> was now to be known as <i>Gigafactory 1</i> as Tesla plans to build more such factories in the future. At the same event, Musk also said that he believed that other companies would build their own similar "Gigafactories".<sup id="cite_ref-TeslaEnergyPresentation1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TeslaEnergyPresentation1-3">[3]</a></sup> After receiving $800M of orders ($179M PowerWall, $625M PowerPack) within 1 week of unveiling, Musk estimated that the Gigafactory 1 is not enough to supply demand.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2016 Elon Musk stated his intention that 'Gigafactory 2' would be located in Europe and manufacture both batteries and complete cars in a location to be chosen in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October_2015_-_Journalist_trespassing">October 2015 - Journalist trespassing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigafactory_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: October 2015 - Journalist trespassing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2015, an altercation between Tesla security staff and two <a href="/wiki/Reno_Gazette-Journal" title="Reno Gazette-Journal">Reno Gazette-Journal</a> journalists occurred when the former responded to a report of photographer Jose Andrews Barron and reporter Jason Hidalgo taking pictures of the factory. While neither Tesla nor the Gazette-Journal dispute Mr. Barron and Mr. Hidalgo being at the site and confronted by Tesla security, the parties do disagree somewhat on what happened subsequently. Tesla alleges that after being confronted, the journalists refused requests to provide their names and IDs and further denied having trespassed despite obviously having passed through a security fence clearly adorned with 'private property' signs. When asked to wait for the authorities, the journalists entered their vehicle and in attempting to leave the scene, allegedly struck several Tesla security employees and a Tesla security ATV. The Gazette-Journal's version alleges that Tesla attempted to actually detain one or both journalists who then had to 'escape' and in doing so may have, because of having to escape, struck one or more Tesla security employees and the aforementioned ATV. In any event, the incident ended with the Sheriff arresting Mr. Barron and charging him with felony battery and trespassing. He was released later that evening on $30,000 bail.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> The felony charges were later dropped in favor of three misdemeanor counts (two for battery and one for trespassing) and set for pre-trial on Feb 4, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> Mr. Hidalgo ended up paying a $195 fine for trespassing.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup></p>
