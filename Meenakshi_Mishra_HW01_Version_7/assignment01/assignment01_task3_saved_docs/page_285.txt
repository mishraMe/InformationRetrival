			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Rebecca Campbell-Howe</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Rebecca (Becky) Campbell-Howe</b> (<a href="/wiki/Floruit" title="Floruit">fl.</a> 2000s; usually writing with her given name reduced to <b>R.</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar-energy</a> editor and non-profit-organization administrator.</p>
<p>As of 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_Campbell-Howe&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, she is Director of Operations of the <a href="/wiki/American_Solar_Energy_Society" title="American Solar Energy Society">American Solar Energy Society</a> (ASES) and is currently Director of Professional Education.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, she received the Colorado Renewable Energy Society's "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cres-energy.org/larson_notari.html">Larson Notari Award</a>" (for significant contributions to the field of renewable energy, energy efficiency, and sustainable buildings).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
