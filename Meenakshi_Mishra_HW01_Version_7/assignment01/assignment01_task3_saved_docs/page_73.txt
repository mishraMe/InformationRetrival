<p><b>The Yazd Solar Power Station</b> is a hybrid combined cycle <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power station</a> situated near <a href="/wiki/Yazd" title="Yazd">Yazd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> which became operational in 2009, and in 2011 as a solar integrated plant. The plant has a capacity of 467&#160;MW and uses <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar energy</a> to augment its steam generation by <a href="/wiki/Concentrating_solar_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Concentrating solar power">concentrating solar power</a> technology.<sup id="cite_ref-mehr130407_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mehr130407-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-payvand130407_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-payvand130407-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tavanir_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tavanir-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
