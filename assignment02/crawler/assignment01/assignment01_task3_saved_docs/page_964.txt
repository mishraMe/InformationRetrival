<p><i><b>The Astrophysical Journal</b></i>, often abbreviated <i><b>ApJ</b></i> (pronounced "ap jay") in references and speech,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> of <a href="/wiki/Astrophysics" title="Astrophysics">astrophysics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>, established in 1895 by American astronomers <a href="/wiki/George_Ellery_Hale" title="George Ellery Hale">George Ellery Hale</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Edward_Keeler" title="James Edward Keeler">James Edward Keeler</a>. The journal discontinued its print edition and became an electronic-only journal in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 1953 <i><b>The Astrophysical Journal Supplement Series</b></i> (<i><b>ApJS</b></i>) has been published in conjunction with <i>The Astrophysical Journal</i>, with generally longer articles to supplement the material in the journal. It publishes six volumes per year, with two 280-page issues per volume.</p>
<p><i><b>The Astrophysical Journal Letters</b></i> (<i><b>ApJL</b></i>) is another section of <i>The Astrophysical Journal</i> which rapidly publishes short communications.</p>
<p>The journal and the supplement series were both published by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago_Press" title="University of Chicago Press">University of Chicago Press</a> for the <a href="/wiki/American_Astronomical_Society" title="American Astronomical Society">American Astronomical Society</a> until, in January 2009, publication was transferred to <a href="/wiki/IOP_Publishing" title="IOP Publishing">IOP Publishing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> following the move of the society's <i><a href="/wiki/Astronomical_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Astronomical Journal">Astronomical Journal</a></i> in 2008. The reason for the changes were given by the society as the increasing financial demands of the University of Chicago Press.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Astrophysical_Journal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal was founded in 1895 by <a href="/wiki/George_Ellery_Hale" title="George Ellery Hale">George Ellery Hale</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_E._Keeler" class="mw-redirect" title="James E. Keeler">James E. Keeler</a> as <i>The Astrophysical Journal: An International Review of Spectroscopy and Astronomical Physics</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition to the two founding editors, there was an international board of associate editors: <a href="/wiki/Marie_Alfred_Cornu" title="Marie Alfred Cornu">M. A. Cornu</a>, Paris; <a href="/wiki/Nils_Christoffer_Dun%C3%A9r" title="Nils Christoffer Dunér">N. C. Dunér</a>, Upsala; <a href="/wiki/William_Huggins" title="William Huggins">William Huggins</a>, London; <a href="/wiki/Pietro_Tacchini" title="Pietro Tacchini">P. Tacchini</a>, Rome; <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Carl_Vogel" title="Hermann Carl Vogel">H. C. Vogel</a>, Potsdam, <a href="/wiki/Charles_S._Hastings" title="Charles S. Hastings">C. S. Hastings</a>, Yale; <a href="/wiki/A._A._Michelson" class="mw-redirect" title="A. A. Michelson">A. A. Michelson</a>, Chicago; <a href="/wiki/Edward_C._Pickering" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward C. Pickering">E. C. Pickering</a>, Harvard; <a href="/wiki/H._A._Rowland" class="mw-redirect" title="H. A. Rowland">H. A. Rowland</a>, Johns Hopkins; and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Augustus_Young" title="Charles Augustus Young">C. A. Young</a>, Princeton.<sup id="cite_ref-Hale1895_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hale1895-6">[6]</a></sup> It was intended that the journal would fill the gap between journals in astronomy and physics, providing a venue for publication of articles on astronomical applications of the <a href="/wiki/Spectroscope" class="mw-redirect" title="Spectroscope">spectroscope</a>; on laboratory research closely allied to astronomical physics, including <a href="/wiki/Wavelength" title="Wavelength">wavelength</a> determinations of metallic and gaseous <a href="/wiki/Spectrum#Electromagnetic_spectrum" title="Spectrum">spectra</a> and experiments on <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">radiation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Absorption_(electromagnetic_radiation)" title="Absorption (electromagnetic radiation)">absorption</a>; on theories of the Sun, Moon, planets, comets, meteors, and nebulae; and on instrumentation for telescopes and laboratories.<sup id="cite_ref-Hale1895_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hale1895-6">[6]</a></sup> The further development of ApJ up to 1995 was outlined by Helmut Abt in an article entitled "Some Statistical Highlights of the <i>Astrophysical Journal</i>" in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
