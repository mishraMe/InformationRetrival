<p>The <b>Longyangxia Dam</b> is a concrete <a href="/wiki/Arch-gravity_dam" title="Arch-gravity dam">arch-gravity dam</a> at the entrance of the Longyangxia canyon on the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gonghe_County" title="Gonghe County">Gonghe County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qinghai_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Qinghai Province">Qinghai Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The dam is 178 metres (584&#160;ft) tall and was built for the purposes of <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a> power generation, irrigation, ice control and flood control. The dam supports a 1,280 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">MW</a> power station with 4 x 320 MW generators that can operate at a maximum capacity of 1400 MW. Controlling ice, the dam controls downstream releases to reservoirs lower in the river, allowing them to generate more power instead of mitigating ice. Water in the dam's 24.7 billion m3 reservoir provides irrigation water for up to 1,000,000 hectares (2,471,054 acres) of land.<sup id="cite_ref-chincold_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chincold-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The dam is composed of its main body and a gravity pier and secondary dam on both its left and right flank. The dam's service <a href="/wiki/Spillway" title="Spillway">spillway</a> contains two 12 metres (39&#160;ft) wide gates that discharge water into two 260 metres (853&#160;ft) and 280 metres (919&#160;ft) long chutes. Downstream discharges are also controlled by a similar single-chute middle outlet and the lower <a href="/wiki/Outlet_works" class="mw-redirect" title="Outlet works">outlet works</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chincold_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chincold-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Photovoltaic_power_station">Photovoltaic power station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longyangxia_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Photovoltaic power station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013 a <a href="/wiki/Solar_photovoltaic" class="mw-redirect" title="Solar photovoltaic">solar photovoltaic</a> station was built with a nameplate capacity of 320 <a href="/wiki/Nominal_power_(photovoltaic)#Watt-peak" title="Nominal power (photovoltaic)">MW<sub>p</sub></a> (Phase I), covering 9 square kilometres (3.5&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> An additional 530 MW<sub>p</sub> (Phase II) was completed in 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-esiafrica_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esiafrica-3">[3]</a></sup> covering further 14 square kilometres (5.4&#160;sq&#160;mi), making Longyangxia Dam Solar Park, with 850 MW<sub>p</sub> capacity, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_photovoltaic_power_stations" title="List of photovoltaic power stations">largest photovoltaic power station in the world</a>.</p>
<p>The solar power station is integrated with the Hydroelectric power station. The park is coupled to one of the hydroelectric turbines, which automatically regulate the output to balance the variable generation from solar before dispatching power to the grid. This limits the problems connected to variable solar generation while helping to conserve water.<sup id="cite_ref-esiafrica_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esiafrica-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
