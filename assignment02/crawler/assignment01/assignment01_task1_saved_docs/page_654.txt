<p>The <b>Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH</b> or <b>GIZ</b> in short (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>German Corporation for International Cooperation GmbH</i> <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[n 1]</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Development_agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Development agency">development agency</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eschborn" title="Eschborn">Eschborn</a> that provides services in the field of <a href="/wiki/Development_cooperation" class="mw-redirect" title="Development cooperation">international development cooperation</a>. GIZ mainly implements technical cooperation projects of the <a href="/wiki/Bundesministerium_f%C3%BCr_wirtschaftliche_Zusammenarbeit_und_Entwicklung" class="mw-redirect" title="Bundesministerium für wirtschaftliche Zusammenarbeit und Entwicklung">Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development</a> (BMZ), its main commissioning party, although it also works with the private sector and other national and supranational government organizations (but usually not with <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organizations" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-governmental organizations">non-governmental organizations</a>) on a public benefit basis. In its activities GIZ seeks to follow the paradigm of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>, which aims at balancing <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> with <a href="/wiki/Social_inclusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Social inclusion">social inclusion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">environmental protection</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> GIZ offers consulting and <a href="/wiki/Capacity_building" title="Capacity building">capacity building</a> services in a wide range of areas, including management consulting, rural development, sustainable infrastructure, security and peace-building, social development, governance and democracy, environment and climate change, and economic development and employment.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>GIZ was established on January 1, 2011 through the merger of three German <a href="/wiki/International_development" title="International development">international development</a> organizations: the <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Entwicklungsdienst" title="Deutscher Entwicklungsdienst">Deutscher Entwicklungsdienst</a> (DED), the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Technische Zusammenarbeit (GTZ), and <a href="/wiki/InWEnt" title="InWEnt">Internationale Weiterbildung und Entwicklung</a> (InWEnt).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> GIZ is one of the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Development_agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Development agency">development agencies</a>, with a business volume in excess of €2.1 billion in 2015 as well as 17,319 employees spread over more than 130 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> Additionally, in cooperation with the German Federal Employment Agency, GIZ operates the Centre for International Migration and Development (CIM), an agency specialized on international cooperation activities related to global labor mobility.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsche_Gesellschaft_f%C3%BCr_Internationale_Zusammenarbeit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GIZ's headquarters are located in <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eschborn" title="Eschborn">Eschborn</a>. It also has a representation in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and offices at 16 other locations across Germany. Outside Germany, the company has a representation in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> and operates 90 offices around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because GIZ is incorporated under German law as a <a href="/wiki/Gesellschaft_mit_beschr%C3%A4nkter_Haftung" title="Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung">GmbH</a> (limited liability company), it is governed by a management board that acts on behalf of the company's shareholders and is monitored by a supervisory board.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> Additionally, GIZ also has a Board of Trustees and a Private Sector Advisory Board.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> GIZ's management board consists of four managing directors and is chaired by Tanja Gönner (January 2017), while the Federal Republic of Germany (represented by the Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ) and the Federal Ministry of Finance (BMF)) is GIZ's sole shareholder.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> The organization is structured into eight corporate units (Corporate Development; Corporate Communications; Legal Affairs and Insurance; Compliance and Integrity; Auditing; Evaluation; Corporate Security; Academy for International Cooperation (AIZ)) and ten departments (Commissioning Parties and Business Development; Sectoral Development; Sector and Global Programmes; Africa; Asia, Latin America, Caribbean; Europe, Mediterranean, Central Asia; International Services; Human Resources).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>GIZ holds a 49% share in sequa gGmbH, the implementing partner of the German business community, in line with the company's objective to foster private sector development and cooperate closely with business chambers and associations abroad. Moreover, GIZ is a member of the European Network of Implementing Development Agencies (EUNIDA), which was co-founded by GTZ in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>GIZ has been involved in the creation of various networks, associations and portals, and may carry out or support secretariat functions for some of these for a limited period of time. Examples of such networks and associations that have had some GIZ involvement include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/REN21" title="REN21">Renewable Energy Policy Network for the 21st Century (REN21)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/BIOPAT_%E2%80%93_Patrons_for_Biodiversity" title="BIOPAT – Patrons for Biodiversity">BIOPAT</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alumniportal_Deutschland" title="Alumniportal Deutschland">Alumniportal Germany</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Sanitation_Alliance" title="Sustainable Sanitation Alliance">Sustainable Sanitation Alliance</a><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Other global agendas supported by GIZ include <a href="/wiki/South-South_cooperation" class="mw-redirect" title="South-South cooperation">South-South cooperation</a>, i.e. bilateral cooperation between developing countries and emerging economies, and triangular cooperation between developing countries as beneficiaries, emerging economies as "new donors" and traditional donors, e.g. Germany, as contributors of expertise.</p>
<p>GIZ works closely with the German government-owned development bank <a href="/wiki/KfW" title="KfW">KfW</a>, which is based in Frankfurt. While GIZ implements those projects on behalf of the BMZ that belong to "technical cooperation", i.e. capacity development, the KfW implements those BMZ projects belonging to "financial cooperation".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>GIZ is currently represented in the SuRe® Stakeholder Council.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> SuRe® – The Standard for Sustainable and Resilient Infrastructure is a global voluntary standard which integrates key criteria of sustainability and resilience into infrastructure development and upgrade. SuRe® is developed by GIB Foundation and Natixis as part of a multi-stakeholder process and will be compliant with ISEAL guidelines.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finally, GIZ also hosts the Eschborn Dialogue, a two-day event that offers international experts a forum to exchange knowledge and experiences on a given topic in international cooperation (e.g. "World in motion: mobility, migration, digital change" in 2014 or "Raw materials and resources: growth, values, competition" in 2013). The Eschborn Dialogue has been organized each year since 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>GIZ mainly operates on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Economic_Cooperation_and_Development" title="Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development">Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development</a> (BMZ). At the national level, GIZ, however, is also commissioned by other government departments, e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Foreign_Office" title="Federal Foreign Office">Federal Foreign Office</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_the_Environment,_Nature_Conservation,_Building_and_Nuclear_Safety" title="Federal Ministry for the Environment, Nature Conservation, Building and Nuclear Safety">Federal Ministry for the Environment</a> (BMUB), or the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Economic_Affairs_and_Energy" title="Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy">Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy</a> (BMWi),<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup> as well as by German states and municipalities.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup> At the international level, GIZ cooperates with the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN agencies</a>, other international institutions such as the <a href="/wiki/Global_Fund_to_Fight_AIDS,_Tuberculosis_and_Malaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria">Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria</a> (GFFATM),<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup> and foreign governments.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup> The cooperation with private enterprises is an emerging field, promoted under the name of sustainable development. The GIZ is set up with International Services (IS) and the <a href="/wiki/Public_Private_Partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Private Partnership">Public Private Partnership</a> (PPP)<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup> in this area.</p>
